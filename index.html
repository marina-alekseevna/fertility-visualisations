Fertility Patterns in England and Wales Over Time

Childbirth is a major event in life, and various factors can influence if and when it takes place. Over the last century many economic and cultural changes occured, and the patterns of fertility followed suit.

Let's take a look at some of the patterns using data from Ons.gov.uk. 2020. Childbearing for women born in different years, England and Wales - Office for National Statistics. [online], which gives us an insight into reproductive choices made by women born between 1920 and 2000.

The heatmap shows either Cumulative or Total Fertility Rate. Cumulative Fertility Rate is the share of women who have had any children, while Total Fertility Rate is ratio of children to women between ages of 16 and 45.
Years (Y axis) show the year of the statistic, which equals to the year of birth of a cohort of women plus their age (X axis).
